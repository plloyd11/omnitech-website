What’s New At OAI

FANUC AUTHORIZED SYSTEM INTEGRATOR

OMNITECH is now an official FANUC ASI

Omnitech Automation, Inc. has been integrating robots for over 25 years and is now also a Fanuc Authorized System Integrator (ASI). As a result, Omnitech will be selling Fanuc robots and parts to end users, designing them into the automated equipment the company designs and builds in-house, and integrating them into equipment at customer facilities. FANUC has the largest offering of robot models with payload capabilities from 0.5 - 2,300 kg. Their robots work in virtually every industry and application including assembly, material handling, material removal, machine tending, packaging, palletizing, painting, dispensing, arc and spot welding. In a recent project, Omnitech integrated four Fanuc robots (2 SCARA, and 2 6-Axis) into a million-dollar+ assembly system designed and fabricated by Omnitech.

NEW HURCO VMX-30i CNC

Omnitech adds a new 3‐Axis Machining Center

To help Omnitech meet its growing demand for machined parts for the Automated Equipment it designs and builds in-house, as well as customer-designed Parts & Fixtures, Omnitech recently replaced an older Bridgeport machining center with a brand new Hurco VM30i. The VM30i has a large 50” x 20” x 20” travel envelope, dual 19” color LCD displays, a Renishaw tool probe, chip auger, and many other time-saving features and capabilities. Using the VM30i’s ethernet port, the machine was connected to Omnitech’s computer network making it quick and easy for a machine operator to import files from Omnitech’s CAM software, thus eliminating the need to manually transfer files or hand-program the machine.

Automation News & Industry 4.0

DESIGNING AUTOMATION FOR THE FUTURE

Smarter Sensors and Devices

As the manufacturing industry moves into the 4th Industrial Revolution, companies are starting to collect more data from equipment on their shop floor. In the past, device technology limitations made it impractical to retrieve information from equipment like stand-alone custom, automated machines. However, new smart devices are providing a path to gathering more information and even sensor-level data and settings. Companies such as ifm efector are upgrading their products to incorporate IO-Link functionality as a standard feature. IO-Link provides a digital communication path to machine sensors, allowing functionality such as monitoring the signal strength of a photoelectric sensor. If the signal falls below a threshold, the controller can send an alert to a maintenance technician to clean the lens or readjust the sensor’s position. Other companies are adding similar component capabilities. For example, SMC Corp of America’s line of smart pneumatic manifolds can count how many times each solenoid is triggered, then send an alert when the solenoid nears its failure threshold. Beyond communicating with engineers, manufacturing and maintenance employees, this new of flow of data can extend all the way to enterprise resource planning (ERP) software systems which can automatically update inventories and provide production data.

Just For Fun

SWEAT-POWERED SMARTWATCHES

The evolution of the power supply

Engineers at the University of Glasgow have developed a new type of flexible supercapacitor, replacing the electrolytes found in conventional batteries with sweat. It can be fully charged with as little as 20 microliters of fluid and is robust enough to survive 4,000 cycles of the types of flexes and bends it might encounter in use. The device works by coating polyester cellulose cloth in a thin layer of a polymer, which acts as the supercapacitor’s electrode. As the cloth absorbs its wearer’s sweat, the positive and negative ions in the sweat interact with the polymer’s surface, creating an electrochemical reaction which generates energy.
